PROGRAM DATES

Morning Session 10.00 am - 12.00 noon & Evening Session 2.00 pm - 4.00 pm

Day 1 - January 02, Tuesday

First video- A BRIEF INTRODUCTION TO THE MAHABHARATA [30 minutes] to be preceded and followed by live interaction

Second Video- A BRIEF INTRODUCTION TO THE MAHABHARATA FESTIVALS OF TAMIL NADU [30 minutes] again to be preceded and followed by a live session

Screening of the film 'KELAI DRAUPADAI'[LISTEN DRAUPADI] followed by a live session.

Kelai Draupadai was awarded the Best Foreign Film at the DIY Film Festival at Los Angeles in 2016 and was the official selection at the Blow Up Chicago International Arthouse Film Festival in 2016

Kelai Draupadai will give the participants of the workshop an overview of the festival whose structure and nature will be elaborated in the following sessions

Day 2 - January 03, Wednesday

Screening of two videos and live interactions

First video- THE IDEA OF THE CHARIOT [34 minutes]

Second video- The Custodians of the Festival [40 minutes]

Screening of two videos and live interactions

First Video- The festivals as 'Total Theatre' [52 minutes]

Second Video- Pottu Raja and the Pallava connection to the festival [57 minutes']

Day 3 - January 04, Thursday

Screening of two videos and live interactions

First video- The Tamil Bharatham- Villibharatham and Nallapillai Bharatham [34 minutes]

Second Video - Chandra Vamsam and the Birth of Vyasa [49 minutes]

Screening of a video followed by interactions

First video- On Koothu as a theatrical form [30 minutes]

Live Koothu performance -Abhimanyu Sandai [90 minutes]

Day 4 - January 05, Friday

Screening of a video followed by a discussion

Video- The first play in the Koothu cycle ‘Vil Valaippu ‘or the ‘Bending of the Bow’in which Arjuna strings the magical bow Kindhooram and wins Draupadi’s hand in marriage [97 minutes]

This Koothu inaugurates the theatre cycle in this festival and from this moment on, each days performance would stretch up to 20 hours a day

Screening of the Koothu ‘ Rajasuya Yagam’ followed by discussion

This Koothu and the following Koothu ‘Pagadai- Thugil’ are the fulcrum around which the festivals have been constructed and in this session the reason for this will be elaborated in the live session and discussion

Day 5 - January 06, Saturday

Screening of the Koothu ‘Pagadai- Thugil’ ot ‘The Game of Dice and the Attempted Disrobing of Draupadi’ followed by discussion

Screening of the video ‘Árjuna's Tapas' [90 minutes] followed by live interaction This is one of the most popular rituals in the festival cycle and people throng in thousands from neighboring villages to participate in this immersive experience.

Day 6 - January 07, Sunday

Screening of the video 'Karna Moksham'[90 minutes] followed by discussion

‘Karna Moksham’ or the ‘Liberation of Karna’ is actually the tragic climax of the festival and the figure of Karna seems to hold multiple resonances for its audiences.

Screening of two videos

First video - The Eoghteenth Day's War and Padugalam [ 90 minutes] where Duryodhana will be finally killed

Second video- Funeral Rites for Duryodhana and the completion of the festival [30 minutes]

While the koothu, the 18th Day’s Battle is treated almost as a comedy, the ritual where the cremation rites for Duryodhana are performed is done in all seriousness. The killing of Duryodhana is visually the most spectacular event in this festival.
